# A STUDY ON AI-DRIVEN BEHAVIORAL ANALYTICS FOR DECISION-MAKING IN BUSINESS ORGANIZATIONS: A PSYCHOLOGICAL AND COMPUTATIONAL APPROACH


A study exploring how **AI-driven behavioral analytics** can enhance managerial decision-making by integrating psychological principles with computational techniques.

---

## üìÑ Overview

This research investigates the role of AI in analyzing human behavior to support decision-making in business organizations. By combining **psychological insights** with **machine learning** and **behavioral analytics**, the study demonstrates how organizations can improve decision quality, productivity, and strategic effectiveness.

**Keywords:** Artificial Intelligence, Behavioral Analytics, Decision-Making, Psychology, Machine Learning, Business Organizations

---

## üß© Conceptual Framework

The study integrates three main components:

1. **Psychological Factors**: Motivation, Stress, Engagement, Cognitive Bias  
2. **AI Techniques**: Machine Learning, Sentiment Analysis, Predictive Modeling  
3. **Decision Outcomes**: Accuracy, Efficiency, Organizational Performance  

AI systems analyze behavioral data influenced by psychological factors to generate actionable insights for managerial decisions.

---

## üõ† Research Methodology

- **Research Design:** Descriptive and analytical, using secondary data and simulated organizational datasets  
- **Sample:** 150 employees from IT, banking, retail, and service sectors  
- **Variables:**
  - Independent: Motivation, Stress Level, Engagement, Behavioral Patterns
  - Dependent: Decision Accuracy, Productivity, Performance Outcomes
- **Tools & Techniques:**
  - Machine Learning classification models
  - Sentiment analysis of employee feedback
  - Statistical analysis of decision outcomes

---

## üìä Key Findings

1. **AI Usage & Decision Accuracy**
   - Low AI: 62% accuracy  
   - Medium AI: 78% accuracy  
   - High AI: 91% accuracy  

2. **Psychological Factors & Productivity**
   - Low Motivation: 4.5/10  
   - Moderate Motivation: 6.9/10  
   - High Motivation: 8.7/10  

3. **Impact:** AI-driven behavioral analytics reduces cognitive bias, improves employee engagement, and enhances strategic alignment.
